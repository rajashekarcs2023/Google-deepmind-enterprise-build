Eleven days after Google I/O 2026, AGI House is partnering with the Google DeepMind team to bring builders into one room to ship real projects on what just launched. Gemini 3.5 Flash, Antigravity 2.0, the Managed Agents API, Gemini Omni Flash, and Science Skills — the most builder-relevant pieces of I/O hit GA or public preview between May 19 and 20. The goal is straightforward — ship something real on the new stack while the engineers who built it are in the room.

Track 1 — Agentic Coding and Builder Tools
Build on Antigravity 2.0, the Managed Agents API, and the Antigravity SDK using Gemini 3.5 Flash. Project space ranges from custom agents that automate specific developer workflows (code migration, security review, on-call response), to MCP servers that expose internal tools, to multi-agent orchestrations that coordinate planning, execution, and verification across subagents. This track is for builders thinking about how software gets built.

Track 2 — Multimodal Agents and Creator Tools
Build with Gemini Omni Flash, Nano Banana, and the Live API to ship creator-facing tools. Project space includes end-to-end content pipelines (brief through assembled video), multimodal product configurators, accessibility tools, and iterative remix-style video editing — anything that exercises the unified text/image/audio/video capabilities Omni introduces. This track is for builders thinking about how content gets made.

Track 3 — AI for Science
Build scientific research agents on Science Skills, Antigravity, and the Managed Agents API. Science Skills ships GA on GitHub and inside Antigravity, integrating 30+ major life-science databases including UniProt, AlphaFold Database, AlphaGenome, InterPro, ClinVar, and OpenAlex. Project space includes structural bioinformatics pipelines, literature synthesis agents, hypothesis exploration workflows, genomic analysis, and agentic workflows that compress scientific work from hours into minutes. This track is for builders working at the boundary of AI and biology, chemistry, medicine, or scientific research more broadly.
